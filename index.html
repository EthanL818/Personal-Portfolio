About me.

Hi! My name is Ethan Liu. I'm a second year software engineering student at Western University in London, Ontario.

I began my programming journey in the sixth grade, where I was introduced to the programming language Scratch. Ever since then, I've developed an interest in the inner workings of everything software related.

"How does this website work?", "How many people did it take to make this game?", "Why does this button do what it does?", etc.

These sorts of questions plagued my mind, up until I decided to pursue software engineering with my post-secondary education. Fast-forward to today, and I'm currently working on developing my fullstack skills.

Recent Developments

My team, out of approximately 130 others, was the only team awarded a certificate of achivement for "Best Team Impression" at the annual ES1050 design showcase.

The annual event is the culmination of a year’s work in the introductory engineering design course ES1050. Throughout the course of the event, guests and participants were asked to vote for their favourite entries.

This year's theme was "accessibility", where each team was assigned a differently abled client to service. Our team (22Treat) was tasked with creating a wheelchair attachable treat dispenser to with the purpose of increasing pet interaction.

Overall, the experience of working together in a group and testing various designs was truly invaluable, and the highlight of my academic year.
